Early presidential elections were held in Turkmenistan <https://
en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkmenistan> on 12 March 2022.^[2] <#cite_note-2>
The elections were called after incumbent president Gurbanguly
Berdimuhamedow <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gurbanguly_Berdimuhamedow>
announced his intention to resign.

The Democratic Party <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/
Democratic_Party_of_Turkmenistan>, which has ruled the country since
independence after the dissolution of the Soviet Union <https://
en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union>, chose Berdimuhamedow's son Serdar
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serdar_Berdimuhamedow> as its candidate.
Serdar Berdimuhamedow was subsequently elected with 73% of the vote.
However, the elections were seen by many international observers as
neither free nor fair <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sham_election>.^[3]
<#cite_note-3> ^[4] <#cite_note-4>


    Background[edit <https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?
    title=2022_Turkmenistan_presidential_election&action=edit&section=1>]

No prior elections in Turkmenistan have been free and fair and the 2022
elections took place in an authoritarian context.^[5] <#cite_note-5>
^[6] <#cite_note-6> ^[7] <#cite_note-7> The country has been described
as a totalitarian <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Totalitarianism>
dictatorship <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dictatorship> under the rule
of Saparmurat Niyazov <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saparmurat_Niyazov>
and Gurbanguly Berdimuhamedow <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/
Gurbanguly_Berdimuhamedow>.^[8] <#cite_note-8> The Democratic Party of
Turkmenistan <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/
Democratic_Party_of_Turkmenistan> is seen as the only legitimate force,
with other parties having been founded after 2012 in order to give the
appearance of a multi-party system.^[9] <#cite_note-9> All legal parties
currently support the government.^[10] <#cite_note-10>

In 2016, the constitution was amended to extend the term of presidents
from five to seven years and to eliminate the age-limit of 70. This was
seen as an effort to allow the incumbent Gurbanguly Berdimuhamedow to
stay in office.^[11] <#cite_note-11> Despite this, he signaled his
resignation just five years into his seven-year term. On 12 February
2022, the Mejlis <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/
Assembly_of_Turkmenistan> passed a resolution scheduling the election
for 12 March in accordance with article 81 of the constitution <https://
en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Turkmenistan>. The son of the
former president, Serdar Berdimuhamedow <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/
Serdar_Berdimuhamedow>, was widely seen as the successor to his
father.^[12] <#cite_note-12>


    Electoral system[edit <https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?
    title=2022_Turkmenistan_presidential_election&action=edit&section=2>]

The President of Turkmenistan <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/
President_of_Turkmenistan> is elected using the two-round system
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two-round_system>^[13] <#cite_note-13>
for a seven-year term.^[14] <#cite_note-14> The minimum age for a
candidate is 40.^[15] <#cite_note-15>

    Campaign[edit <https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?
    title=2022_Turkmenistan_presidential_election&action=edit&section=4>]

The campaign stage began on 21 February.^[25] <#cite_note-25> In
accordance with Article 51 of the Electoral Code of Turkmenistan,
candidates from the moment of their registration have equal rights to
speak at pre-election meetings, campaign meetings, and in the media.
Bodies of state power and local self-government, officials of
institutions, organizations and enterprises are obligated to assist
candidates in obtaining the necessary information related to
elections.^[26] <#cite_note-:0-26>

According to Article 52 of the Electoral Code of Turkmenistan, each
candidate for the President of Turkmenistan can have up to three proxies
in each etrap <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Districts_of_Turkmenistan>
and each city with the rights of an etrap.^[26] <#cite_note-:0-26>
Candidate meetings throughout the territory of Turkmenistan are held
with broad participation of the public, representatives of labor
collectives of enterprises and organizations, honorary elders and
youth.^[27] <#cite_note-auto4-27> ^[28] <#cite_note-28> ^[29]
<#cite_note-29>


    Conduct[edit <https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?
    title=2022_Turkmenistan_presidential_election&action=edit&section=5>]

On 28 February 2022, an observer mission from the Commonwealth of
Independent States <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/
Commonwealth_of_Independent_States> (CIS) arrived in Turkmenistan.^[30]
<#cite_note-30> ^[31] <#cite_note-31> Early voting began on 2 March,
where a total of 41 polling stations were opened in 30 countries.^[32]
<#cite_note-32> ^[33] <#cite_note-33> Turnout to the election was
reported at 97%, according to the Central Election Committee.^[34]
<#cite_note-34>


    Results[edit <https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?
    title=2022_Turkmenistan_presidential_election&action=edit&section=6>]

The Central Election Committee reported on 15 March that Serdar
Berdimuhamedow won the election with 72.97% of the vote. University
official Hydyr Nunnaýew came in second place with 11.09%. Unlike in
prior elections, the preliminary results were not available on the day
after the election. Berdimuhamedow received the lowest number of votes
out of any winning candidate in all Turkmen presidential elections.^[35]

    Reactions[edit <https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?
    title=2022_Turkmenistan_presidential_election&action=edit&section=7>]

General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party <https://
en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party>
Xi Jinping <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping> sent a message of
congratulations to Serdar Berdimuhamedow after the results were
announced.^[36] <#cite_note-36> Russian President <https://
en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Russia> Vladimir Putin <https://
en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Putin> reportedly extended his
congratulations in a phone call with both Gurbanguly and Serdar
Berdimuhamedow.^[37] <#cite_note-37> The Organization of Turkic States
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organization_of_Turkic_States>
congratulated him via Twitter <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/
Twitter>.^[38] <#cite_note-38>
